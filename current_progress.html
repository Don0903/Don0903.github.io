Ongoing projects


In 2016, only 20% of all e-waste was recycled, be part of the change: support ongoing projects! Different initiatives exist to encourage people to recycle their phones, here are two examples

They're calling on you

Achievement: They have recycled and re-purposed more than 180 000 devices.

Problem: Coltan is a metal used in mobile phones whose mining represents one of the biggest threats to Eastern Lowland Gorillas, since it implies destroying their habitat. Without a place to live, those gorillas are expected to become extinct.

Solution:The zoos Victoria Community reclaims over 95% of the materials of mobile phones that are recycled in order to preserve as much as possible the gorillas habitat since mining of raw materials can be avoided, and at the same time by preserving the ecosystems since mobile phones are kept out of landfill.

Further details: An estimate of how phone collections can help wild gorillas is provided on their website.

Close the loop

Achievement: They won the Circular Award in 2018 and many others.

Problem: In 2016, 44.7 million metric tons of e-waste were generated according to a report of the International Solid Waste Association. A phone's birth is the most contaminating part of its life cycle: around 80% of each device's carbon footprint is generated at the manufacturing stage. In addition, the extraction and processing of virgin materials such as precious metals include a large land use as well as water and air pollution.

Solution: The transition to a circular economy can bring numerous benefits, since it focuses on extending the life cycle of products, helping to minimize waste. Therefore, pressure on the environment would be reduced and the availability of raw materials would be optimized. Close the loop represents the circular economy in action, by offering companies the possibility to offset the impact of their phones. For every phone that companies buy for their employees, Closing the Loop recovers a scrap phone from Africa or Asia so that the newly bought phones become material neutral and they are not contributing to e-waste.

further details: a precise description of how they make a difference is provided on their website.

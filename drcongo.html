Child-labour in DR Congo


From a report done by the "Worker members" in 2017 International Law Commission, the state of child labour in Dominican Republic of Congo was highlighted to be very distresing.

They stated:

"Children were also exposed to the worst forms of child labour in the mines of Katanga and East Kasai, where around 40,000 children were working under the oversight of military units on mineral extraction. They worked in mines for up to 12 hours a day, for US$1 or $2, in extremely hot temperatures, without the slightest protection and in contact with high concentrations of cobalt"

Got pollution?

5 billion people were estimated to have a phone in 2020, and the number has been growing since. This represents an environmental disaster, since mining to obtain the needed materials destroys ecosystems and generates toxic by-products. The atmosphere is contaminated and natural resources are depleted. When extracting metals from the earth, enormous volumes of solid and liquid waste are produced and many lands are deforested. Consequences of these unsustainable practices are catastrophic events that happen all around the world, from Brazil, to Indonesia, to China.

The metals extracted are a precious and finite resource, which therefore needs to be preserved. In addition, toxic metals in phones will continue to contaminate water and soil if they end up in landfill sites.
